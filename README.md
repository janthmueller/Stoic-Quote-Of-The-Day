    <em><!--START_SECTION:quote-text-->
Be aware, then, that every human condition is subject to change, and that whatever mishap can befall any man can also happen to you.
<!--END_SECTION:quote-text--></em><br>
    <strong>— <!--START_SECTION:quote-author-->
Seneca
<!--END_SECTION:quote-author--></strong>
</p>

<div align="center" style="max-width:600px;margin:0 auto;">
<!--START_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
Life is full of unpredictable changes and challenges, and recognizing this can help us navigate it with greater resilience and empathy. In today’s fast-paced world, people often strive for stability and predictability, seeking to avoid any disruption to their well-laid plans. However, just as Seneca reminds us, every situation we find ourselves in is impermanent, and no one is immune to life’s ups and downs. Imagine someone thriving in their career today and unexpectedly facing redundancy tomorrow due to unforeseen economic shifts. Such experiences are reminders that, as much as we might try to control our circumstances, change is inevitable. By accepting the mutable nature of life, we cultivate a mindset that prepares us for whatever comes our way. This understanding also fosters compassion, as we relate to others' struggles, knowing we all share vulnerability to life’s unpredictability. Embodying this insight, we are less likely to be caught off-guard by adversity or overly reliant on the illusion of permanence, instead developing flexibility and a deeper sense of community with those around us.
