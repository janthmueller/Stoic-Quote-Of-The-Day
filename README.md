    <em><!--START_SECTION:quote-text-->
Don't live by your own rules, but in harmony with nature.
<!--END_SECTION:quote-text--></em><br>
    <strong>— <!--START_SECTION:quote-author-->
Epictetus
<!--END_SECTION:quote-author--></strong>

<div align="center" style="max-width:600px;margin:0 auto;">
<!--START_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
When Epictetus advises not to live by our own rules but in harmony with nature, he's encouraging us to align our lives with the natural order and rational structure of the world. In contemporary terms, this suggests embracing the understanding that there are aspects of life beyond our control and finding peace within those boundaries. Imagine standing in traffic, feeling the frustration mount as you’re stuck waiting. You could rail against the reality of the situation, insisting it’s unfair or unnecessary. Alternatively, by recognizing traffic as a natural occurrence in a bustling cityscape and accepting its inconveniences as part of modern life, you free yourself from unnecessary agitation. This acceptance isn’t passive resignation; it’s an active choice to focus your energy on what you can control—your reactions, your perspective, and how you utilize the time at hand. Rather than being tied down by personal expectations or rigid plans, living in harmony with nature means observing the unchangeable elements around you and adapting with grace and resilience. It’s about finding balance, much like a tree that bends with the wind to remain unbroken, allowing you to navigate life's unpredictability with equanimity and wisdom.
