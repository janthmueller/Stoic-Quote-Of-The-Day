<p align="center">
    <em><!--START_SECTION:quote-text-->
Not to live as if you had endless years ahead of you. Death overshadows you. While you’re alive and able—be good.
<!--END_SECTION:quote-text--></em><br>
    <strong>— <!--START_SECTION:quote-author-->
Marcus Aurelius
<!--END_SECTION:quote-author--></strong>
</p>

<p align="center" style="max-width:600px;margin:0 auto;">
<!--START_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
Marcus Aurelius advises us to recognize that our time on Earth is limited. Instead of living with the illusion of endless years stretching ahead, we should acknowledge the inevitability of death, which serves as a powerful reminder to make the most of the present. By understanding that life is transient, we can prioritize what truly matters, such as virtuous actions, meaningful relationships, and personal growth. In today’s fast-paced world, this perspective encourages mindfulness and intentionality. For instance, instead of procrastinating on expressing gratitude to loved ones or delaying pursuits of passion, we are inspired to seize the day, making kindness and integrity guiding principles in our daily interactions. This mindset helps us focus on living a fulfilling life characterized by deeds that contribute to our personal well-being and leave a positive impact on others, emphasizing the importance of virtue and goodness while we have the ability and opportunity.
<!--END_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
</p>
