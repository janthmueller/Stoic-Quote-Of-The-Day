<p align="center">
    <em><!--START_SECTION:quote-text-->
Give up your thirst for books, so that you do not die a grouch.
<!--END_SECTION:quote-text--></em><br>
    <strong>— <!--START_SECTION:quote-author-->
Marcus Aurelius
<!--END_SECTION:quote-author--></strong>
</p>

<div align="center" style="max-width:600px;margin:0 auto;">
<!--START_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
The core idea here is not to abandon reading altogether, but rather to caution against an excessive or obsessive pursuit of knowledge for its own sake without applying it meaningfully in your life. Marcus Aurelius, a Roman emperor known for his practical philosophy, suggests that hoarding knowledge without acting on it can lead to dissatisfaction or bitterness. Imagine someone who spends all their time collecting books or endlessly scrolling through newsfeeds and articles, seeking more information without ever using what they've learned to improve their life or interact with the world around them. By reminding us to focus on how we can apply wisdom, rather than just accumulating facts, the quote encourages us to live in accordance with what we learn, letting our actions reflect our understanding. This way, we cultivate a sense of fulfillment and purpose, avoiding the frustration that comes from knowing much but doing little. In our fast-paced digital age, where information is abundant, it's a gentle nudge to balance learning with living, ensuring our pursuit of knowledge enriches both our personal growth and connections with others.
