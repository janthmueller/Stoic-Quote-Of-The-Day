<p align="center">
    <em><!--START_SECTION:quote-text-->
Luck is what happens when preparation meets opportunity!
<!--END_SECTION:quote-text--></em><br>
    <strong>— <!--START_SECTION:quote-author-->
Seneca
<!--END_SECTION:quote-author--></strong>
</p>

<p align="center" style="max-width:600px;margin:0 auto;">
<!--START_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
In life, we often attribute success to luck, as if it were a random gift bestowed by fate. However, Seneca offers a deeper perspective, suggesting that what we call luck is actually the result of being prepared for the moments when opportunities arise. Imagine you're an aspiring musician practicing diligently for years; when the chance to perform before a record producer unexpectedly arrives, your readiness allows you to seize it. Without preparation, such moments might pass unnoticed or ungrasped. Similarly, a student who consistently studies will find that when a surprise pop quiz is given, their preparation turns what could be a stressful situation into an opportunity to excel. In our careers, relationships, or creative pursuits, being prepared doesn’t just improve our skills; it tunes our perception to recognize possibilities others might miss, transforming mere chance into calculated success. Therefore, embracing a mindset of continuous learning and readiness can empower us to make the most of life's unpredictable offerings.
<!--END_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
</p>
