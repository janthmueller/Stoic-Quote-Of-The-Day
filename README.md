    <em><!--START_SECTION:quote-text-->
Nothing is ours, except time.
<!--END_SECTION:quote-text--></em><br>
    <strong>— <!--START_SECTION:quote-author-->
Seneca
<!--END_SECTION:quote-author--></strong>
</p>

<div align="center" style="max-width:600px;margin:0 auto;">
<!--START_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
Seneca reminds us that while we may strive to amass wealth, possessions, and status, the only true asset we genuinely possess is time. In a world where every minute feels like a race against responsibilities and ambitions, time stands alone as the single currency beyond external influence or ownership transfer. Yet, ironically, it's often the most undervalued resource, slipping through our fingers in the guise of procrastination, mindless distractions, or overcommitment to obligations that bring little joy or meaning. Consider the office worker who sacrifices evenings to check emails, or the student who scrolls endlessly through social media instead of savoring their education; these are familiar scenarios where time is spent without conscious choice. By realizing that time is our most authentic possession, we’re encouraged to invest it wisely, focusing on pursuits and people that enrich our lives and nurture our spirit. It calls on us to be present, deliberate, and selective with how we allocate our time, ensuring that the ways we spend this invaluable resource reflect our true values and priorities.
