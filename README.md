<p align="center">
    <em><!--START_SECTION:quote-text-->
Happiness and freedom begin with a clear understanding of one principle: Some things are within our control, and some things are not.
<!--END_SECTION:quote-text--></em><br>
    <strong>— <!--START_SECTION:quote-author-->
Epictetus
<!--END_SECTION:quote-author--></strong>
</p>

<div align="center" style="max-width:600px;margin:0 auto;">
<!--START_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
At the heart of Epictetus' insight is the recognition of control: distinguishing between what we can influence and what lies beyond our reach. In everyday life, this principle guides us toward inner peace and true freedom by encouraging us to focus our energy and efforts solely on what we can change. Imagine the stress often felt when waiting for a job interview result or a loved one's text message response—these outcomes are uncontrollable and subject to many external factors. By accepting this, we can redirect our attention to aspects we can control, like crafting a thoughtful cover letter for the next opportunity or composing a heartfelt message. Such discernment liberates us from fruitless worry about externals and empowers us to shape our character and responses, leading to a more balanced and content life. Embracing this simple yet profound wisdom allows us to free ourselves from unnecessary anxieties, anchoring our happiness in what truly depends on us: our actions, thoughts, and attitudes.
<!--END_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
