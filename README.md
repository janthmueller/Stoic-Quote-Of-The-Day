<p align="center">
    <em><!--START_SECTION:quote-text-->
Remember: Matter. How tiny your share of it. Time. How brief and fleeting your allotment of it. Fate. How small a role you play in it.
<!--END_SECTION:quote-text--></em><br>
    <strong>— <!--START_SECTION:quote-author-->
Marcus Aurelius
<!--END_SECTION:quote-author--></strong>
</p>

<div align="center" style="max-width:600px;margin:0 auto;">
<!--START_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
Marcus Aurelius invites us to contemplate our place within the vast tapestry of the universe, encouraging a perspective rooted in humility and awareness. By reflecting on "matter," we are reminded of our physical existence's insignificance compared to the infinite cosmos. This realization can help us focus on maximizing the use of what we have, regardless of its size. Considering "time," we understand that our journey on Earth is fleeting, urging us to cherish each moment, prioritize what truly matters, and not squander time on trivial pursuits. In our contemporary lives, this might mean valuing quality time with loved ones over material possessions or personal achievements. The reflection on "fate" and the small role we play in it teaches us acceptance. We cannot control everything that happens in life; sometimes, we must let go and trust the natural course of events. In today’s world, this can translate into handling unexpected changes with grace and detachment, recognizing that there are forces beyond our control. Embracing these insights can lead to a peaceful, grounded existence, allowing us to live fully in the present and act with intention and purpose.
