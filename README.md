    <em><!--START_SECTION:quote-text-->
If anyone can refute me—show me I’m making a mistake or looking at things from the wrong perspective—I’ll gladly change. It’s the truth I’m after, and the truth never harmed anyone. What harms us is to persist in self-deceit and ignorance.
<!--END_SECTION:quote-text--></em><br>
    <strong>— <!--START_SECTION:quote-author-->
Marcus Aurelius
<!--END_SECTION:quote-author--></strong>
</p>

<div align="center" style="max-width:600px;margin:0 auto;">
<!--START_SECTION:quote-interpretation-->
Embracing the wisdom of Marcus Aurelius means being open to correction and understanding that acknowledging when we're wrong is a valuable path to personal growth. In our daily lives, we often find ourselves steadfast in our perspectives, sometimes out of pride or a desire to be seen as right. However, true strength lies in our willingness to change our viewpoints when presented with evidence or reasoning that our initial outlook was flawed. Consider a workplace scenario where a colleague points out an error in your report. Instead of reacting defensively, taking a step back and assessing the situation objectively can lead to improved results and teamwork. By seeking the truth rather than clinging to mistaken beliefs, we cultivate a mindset that values clarity over stubbornness. Persisting in self-deceit only leads to stagnant development and potential relational strains. Adopting a posture of openness not only leads to personal enlightenment but also enriches our interactions with others, fostering an environment where truth is prioritized over ego. In essence, real freedom and peace come from knowing that learning and changing are not signs of weakness, but rather indicators of a resilient and wise character.
